Our goal for this project was to design a web experience that reconciles both the digital and analog worlds of shopping by facilitating social interaction among friends.

Through our research, we learned about what shoppers are looking for when they decide to physically go to a mall to shop versus shopping online. We learned more about the nuances of shopping with friends and asking for friends’ opinions.

From this, we developed MallGo.com, a way for friends to shop together in a mall setting while they were shopping online.

About Me
I have always worked on things to improve people's lives - from volunteering with Key Club in high school to working on improving financial software interfaces today, I enjoy helping people and being involved. While at Carnegie Mellon University, I learned how to use my love of computers and technology to make people's lives easier through user experience design. I learned how to create designs based on user research, and am now able to craft experiences that meet the ultimate goal of users. Between my involvement in various organizations here in New York and studying Human-Computer Interaction, I have found my passion in creating experiences for people, be it through planning vacations with friends, planning Startup Weekend events, or improving the user experience of financial software.
