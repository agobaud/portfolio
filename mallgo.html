Our goal for this project was to design a web experience that reconciles both the digital and analog worlds of shopping by facilitating social interaction among friends.
Through our research, we learned about what shoppers are looking for when they decide to physically go to a mall to shop versus shopping online. We learned more about the nuances of shopping with friends and asking for friends’ opinions.
From this, we developed MallGo.com, a way for friends to shop together in a mall setting while they were shopping online.

About Me
I am a 23 year old currently living in New York City with a passion for technology and design. After graduating from Carnegie Mellon University in May 2012 with a degree in Information Systems and Human-Computer Interaction, I began working with Deutsche Bank first as a User Experience Analyst, and currently as a Business Analyst.

While I’ve learned a lot from working in the financial industry, I am ready to get back to what I am most interested in: user experience and interaction design. I am currently looking for a full time position using not only the skills I learned at Carnegie Mellon, but also using the skills I picked up while working at an investment bank for the past year.

In my spare time, I enjoy reading, hiking, pretending to be a distance runner, hosting garden parties, and exploring all that New York has to offer.
