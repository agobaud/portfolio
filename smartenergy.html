Project Background
The Smart Energy Visualization project was a collaborative project between Bosch Research and the Carnegie Mellon Undergraduate Human-Computer Interaction department.
Through our research, we discovered what was most relevant to users’ energy consumption. From there, we sought to create an actionable interface that increases energy awareness for users and built our skill sets in user research, data visualization, and overall interface usability. We determined a target population of interest and created an information display meant to influence the users’ behavior related to energy conservation.
Our project goal was to make use of Bosch’s non-intrusive load monitoring sensors to provide bill-payers with actionable information on their energy usage. We initially focused on home owners, landlords and facility managers, however based on the results of our user research, we narrowed our audience to home owners.
We gathered information to evaluate the incentives users desire through interviews and discovered what information is most actionable, leading to a refined specificity of the information they require. This guided our design of Smart Energy.
The Solution
Smart Energy is a desktop and mobile website designed to allow users to view their energy usage throughout their home, and encourage users to minimize their energy usage. With various visualizations comparing energy usage on a daily, weekly, and monthly basis, users could see not only how much total energy they were using, butalso how much energy each appliance was using. If an appliance was using an abnormally large amount of energy,the user would be notified to check on the status of that appliance, in case it was broken.
More information about the project, our research, and our solution can be found here in our Final Report.
